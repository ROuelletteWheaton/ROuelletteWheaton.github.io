Creating the Board

The process of making the boards for our signs was one of the most unique and enjoyable projects of the class for me, simply because it was something I previously had no knowledge of. Though the process was a bit confusing, and the board I made ended up not being used in favor of the one Tuna made, it was still cool to do. Below are pictures from the process of making the board, the settings to input into the drill and the drill itself, as well as the assembly and soldering of the board.

Creating the Sign

I had a fair bit of trouble coming up with a desing for the sign for this project. Originally I was going to do something connected to the museum I volunteer at, but I had trouble implementing my design, so instead I opted to use a design from a stylized graphic for Halo. I imported the image into CSP, and later into AI, before cutting it on the Glowforge.
